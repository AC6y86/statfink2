# Week 5, 2025: The Massacre at Fantasy Gulch

*The wind howled through the abandoned streets of Fantasy Gulch. Tumbleweeds rolled past the saloon doors. Somewhere in the distance, a church bell tolled five times. Week Five had arrived, and with it, the kind of violence that makes even hardened men look away.*

## High Noon at the Crossroads

The sun hung merciless in the October sky, casting long shadows across the dusty main street where twelve gunslingers had gathered for another week of bloodshed. These weren't your ordinary desperados - no sir, these were fantasy owners, the most dangerous breed of outlaw in all the territories. Each one armed with nineteen bullets in their chamber, ready to draw at the first sign of Sunday morning.

Old-timer Cal stood on the porch of the general store, spitting tobacco juice into the dirt. His 110.3 points per week average made him one of the most feared guns in these parts, though this week he'd taste defeat. Chris, the fastest draw in Fantasy Gulch with his 114.0 average, cleaned his weapons methodically, knowing that consistency was worth more than gold in these lands. And then there was Joe - averaging 119.7 points a week, the kind of numbers that made grown men whisper prayers and check their life insurance policies.

But averages don't mean much when the clock strikes noon and the shootin' starts. In this godforsaken wasteland, anything could happen. Heroes could fall. Cowards could rise. And sometimes, just sometimes, a massacre so brutal would occur that they'd speak of it in hushed tones for generations to come.

## The Slaughter at Mitch's Ranch: Joe's 150.0 to Mitch's 58.5

*The dust hadn't even settled from the morning dew when the first shots rang out.*

They say you could hear the screams from three counties over. What Joe did to Mitch that Sunday afternoon wasn't just a victory - it was a public execution, the kind they used to do in the town square to make an example of horse thieves and card cheats. A 91.5-point differential that left blood soaking into the desert sand.

Joe rode into battle with a posse that would make the James Gang jealous. Jonathan Taylor, that Indianapolis stallion, galloped for 29.0 hard-earned points, trampling everything in his path like a man possessed by demons. Justin Fields added 23.0 points of his own, slinging leather with the precision of a seasoned gunfighter. Rico Dowdle matched Fields bullet for bullet with another 23.0, while Mac Jones contributed 22.0 points - four quarterbacks dealing death from every angle, a strategy as unconventional as it was devastating.

"Weren't natural," muttered an old prospector who witnessed the carnage. "Four quarterbacks... it's like bringing a Gatling gun to a knife fight."

Poor Mitch. Lord have mercy on poor Mitch. His top gun, Kareem Hunt, managed only 16.0 points - respectable on any other day, but on this day, it was like bringing a derringer to defend against cannon fire. Puka Nacua tried to help with 14.0 points, but it was like trying to stop a flash flood with a tablespoon. The rest of Mitch's gang - Stefon Diggs with 9.0, Xavier Legette with 8.0 - they fought bravely, but bravery don't mean much when you're outgunned three-to-one.

The final insult came from Mitch's bottom dwellers. Cooper Kupp, once a legendary gunslinger, managed only 3.0 points. Keenan Allen, another faded star, matched that pitiful 3.0. Brian Robinson Jr., Samaje Perine, and the Vikings defense? They never even drew their weapons, scoring zeros that echoed through the canyon like a death rattle. When the smoke cleared and the bodies were counted, Mitch had scored just 58.5 points - the kind of total that makes a man consider hanging up his spurs for good.

This wasn't just the biggest blowout of Week 5; this was the kind of beating that changes a man. They found Mitch later that evening, sitting alone in the saloon, staring at his whiskey like it held answers to questions he was afraid to ask. His season average of 86.5 points - already the worst in Fantasy Gulch - had taken another hit. Some say he hasn't been the same since.

## The Shootout at Dan's Corral: Matt's 122.0 to Dan's 76.0

*As the sun climbed higher, casting shorter shadows, another duel was taking shape.*

Matt rode into Dan's territory with bad intentions and a loaded roster. His 122.0 points came like thunder across the prairie - sudden, violent, and impossible to ignore. C.J. Stroud led the charge with 26.0 points, his arm as true as any rifle barrel ever forged. Christian McCaffrey, that San Francisco legend, added 17.0 points of pure destruction, while Rashid Shaheed matched him with another 17.0, dancing through defenses like a ghost through graveyard gates.

Even Matt's backup, young Bryce Young, contributed 16.0 points - the kind of depth that separates the survivors from the deceased in these harsh lands. It was a balanced attack, each player doing their part, like a well-rehearsed bank heist where everyone knows their role and executes it perfectly.

Dan fought back with everything he had, but 76.0 points in this environment was like showing up to a gunfight already bleeding. Kyren Williams managed 22.0 points, fighting with the desperation of a cornered animal. Jalen Hurts added 19.0, but you could see it in his eyes - he knew the battle was lost before the fourth quarter sun had set.

The 46-point defeat left Dan's crew scattered across the battlefield like broken wagon wheels. His season average of 94.3 points per week had promised more, but promises don't mean much when Matt's rolling into town with a 122-point arsenal. This was Matt's finest hour of the season, a performance that elevated his average to 93.0 points per week and served notice to the rest of Fantasy Gulch: underestimate Matt at your own peril.

"That boy Matt," said an old bartender, wiping down glasses with a dirty rag, "he picked his moment perfect-like. When Dan was vulnerable, he struck like a rattlesnake - fast, deadly, and without mercy."

## The Ambush at Bruce's Hideout: Bruce's 98.0 to Eli's 79.5

*The afternoon sun beat down merciless as Bruce set his trap.*

Bruce had been waiting for this moment. His 98.0-point ambush caught Eli's gang flat-footed, despite Eli carrying two loaded quarterbacks into battle. Patrick Mahomes delivered 25.0 points with the reliability of a Swiss pocket watch, while Tua Tagovailoa added 24.0 points of his own. Fifty points from the quarterback position alone - in most fights, that's enough to walk away victorious.

But Bruce had Ja'Marr Chase, and that changed everything. Chase's 25.0 points came like lightning strikes - sudden, brilliant, and devastating. Combined with David Montgomery's steady 16.0 points, Bruce had built a fortress that Eli's 79.5 points couldn't breach.

The 18.5-point defeat stung all the more because Eli had come prepared. His weapons were loaded, his strategy was sound, but sometimes in the desert, the better-prepared man still dies of thirst. Eli's season average of 91.2 points suggested he should have won this fight, but Bruce's 99.3 average proved that consistency beats potential every time the sun sets on Sunday.

"Three players on injured reserve," Eli would later lament, staring at the medical reports. Joe Mixon (Out), Omarion Hampton (Out), and Trey Benson (Injured Reserve) - his depth decimated like a cavalry unit after a failed charge. "Hard to win a war when half your soldiers are in the infirmary."

## The Quickdraw at Noon: Mike's 100.0 to Aaron's 89.5

*Two gunslingers faced each other on Main Street. The crowd held its breath.*

Mike reached the century mark - 100.0 points exactly, like hitting the bullseye from fifty paces while riding backwards on a spooked horse. Jacory Croskey-Merritt emerged from nowhere to score 25.0 points, the kind of performance that makes unknown outlaws into legends overnight. Baker Mayfield added his steady 24.0 points, reliable as sunrise in the desert.

Aaron fought with honor, his 89.5 points including Javonte Williams' surprising 25.0-point explosion and Jaxson Dart's 19.0-point contribution. But when the smoke cleared, Mike stood victorious by 10.5 points. Not a massacre, not a blowout - just a good, clean kill. The kind that earns respect in these parts.

"That Croskey-Merritt boy," whispered the townspeople, "where'd Mike find him? Pulled 25.0 points out of thin air like some kind of sorcery."

Aaron's season average of 80.9 points - dead last in Fantasy Gulch - meant this loss hurt less than it might have. When you're already at the bottom of the canyon, another tumble don't change the view much. But Mike's 90.7 average got a much-needed boost, keeping him in the hunt for respectability as the season wore on.

## The Knife Fight in the Alley: Chris's 98.0 to Sean's 91.0

*In the shadows between buildings, two veterans settled old scores.*

Chris and Sean went at each other like wildcats in a burlap sack - close, vicious, and personal. Chris's 98.0 points came from a perfectly orchestrated attack. Sam Darnold, that Seattle gunslinger, led the charge with an incredible 32.0 points, the highest individual score of the entire week. Dak Prescott backed him up with 26.0 points, while Josh Allen added 22.0 more. Three quarterbacks combining for 80 points - it was like watching a man fight with three guns at once, one in each hand and one between his teeth.

Sean countered with everything he had. Jaylen Waddle contributed 17.0 points, Rhamondre Stevenson added 16.0, but his 91.0-point total fell just short. Seven points - in a league where 91.5 points could win by a landslide or 91.0 could lose a nail-biter, such is the cruel nature of Fantasy Gulch.

"If Sam Darnold hadn't gone completely loco with those 32.0 points," Sean would say later, nursing his wounds, "that fight could've gone either way."

The defeat dropped Sean's average to 89.4 points per week, while Chris maintained his position as one of the most feared gunslingers in town with his 114.0 average. Sometimes the rich get richer, and the poor get buried in unmarked graves.

## The Last Stand at Sunset: Pete's 96.5 to Cal's 94.0

*As the sun began its descent toward the horizon, the closest fight of the day unfolded.*

This was a battle of attrition, decided by the slimmest of margins - 2.5 points. The kind of difference that could come down to a single broken tackle or a field goal drifting wide. Pete's 96.5 points included a quarterback trinity of his own: Matthew Stafford with 27.0, Jared Goff with 24.0, and Jake Browning with 24.0. Seventy-five points from the quarterback position - in the new age of Fantasy Gulch, it seemed every successful outlaw was running multiple signal-callers.

Cal fought with the fury of a man who knew his 110.3 season average demanded better. Trevor Lawrence delivered 30.0 massive points, the second-highest individual score of the week. Emeka Egbuka added 22.0 points, while Jaxon Smith-Njigba contributed 17.0. But it wasn't enough. Not quite.

The 2.5-point margin was so thin you could see through it like cheesecloth. Cal's two injured reserve players - Braelon Allen and Malik Nabers, both lost for extended time - had left holes in his roster that even his impressive remaining firepower couldn't quite fill.

"Two and a half points," Cal muttered, kicking the dirt. "Might as well have been a hundred."

Pete's victory pushed his average to 96.0 points per week, while Cal's 110.3 average took a hit it didn't deserve. Sometimes in the desert, the better man loses. That's what makes it cruel.

## The Tiers of the Damned

As the dust settled on Week 5, the gunfighters of Fantasy Gulch could be separated into distinct castes, like layers of sediment in a canyon wall:

**The Elite (110+ points average):** Joe stood alone at the summit with his 119.7 average, Chris followed at 114.0, and Cal held strong at 110.3 despite this week's narrow defeat. These were the men who made widows and orphans, the ones decent folk crossed the street to avoid.

**The Competitive (95-105 points average):** Bruce (99.3), Pete (96.0), and barely clinging on, Dan (94.3) and Matt (93.0). These fighters could take down anyone on their day, dangerous enough to fear but not quite elite enough to rule.

**The Struggling (85-95 points average):** Eli (91.2), Mike (90.7), Sean (89.4), and poor Mitch (86.5). They won some and lost more, always fighting uphill, always one bad week from complete collapse.

**The Basement Dweller (Below 85):** Aaron, alone at 80.9 points per week, wondering if this wasteland was worth the suffering.

## Tales from the Bust Brigade

They lined up at dawn, supposed stars who fired blanks when it mattered most. Cooper Kupp and Keenan Allen, once-mighty gunslingers, managed only 3.0 points each for Mitch - their barrels rusted, their aim gone. Christian Kirk put up the same pitiful 3.0 for Joe, though Joe had enough firepower elsewhere to survive the disappointment.

Zay Flowers wilted to 3.0 points for Dan, while Chris Olave managed the same embarrassment for Sean. These weren't rookies making rookie mistakes - these were veterans who'd forgotten how to fight, their reputations worth more than their current production.

"Time comes for every gunfighter," the undertaker said, measuring them for pine boxes. "Some just don't know when to hang up their holsters."

## The Close Calls and What-Ifs

In a week dominated by blowouts, only one fight truly went down to the wire. Pete's 2.5-point victory over Cal was decided by margins so thin that a single play could have changed everything. If Trevor Lawrence's 30.0 points had been 33.0, if one field goal had gone through instead of wide, if the wind had blown differently...

"That's the cruelest kind of loss," Cal said, staring at the horizon. "The kind where you did everything right and still came up short."

But there were no other close calls this week. Every other fight was decided by double digits, most by landslides. It was a week of absolutes, of dominance and destruction, where the strong feasted and the weak were devoured.

## Looking Toward the Horizon: The Road Ahead

As Week 6 approaches, the landscape of Fantasy Gulch has been forever altered. Joe's massacre of Mitch will be remembered as one of the most brutal displays of dominance in recent memory. Matt proved he could compete with the big guns when everything clicked. Chris and Joe continued to separate themselves from the pack, while Aaron and Mitch found themselves staring up from the bottom of very deep holes.

The injured reserve list grows longer with each passing week. Three-quarterback strategies have become the new gospel. And somewhere out there in the desert, Mitch is still trying to figure out how he scored only 58.5 points with a full roster, still seeing Joe's 150.0 points in his nightmares.

"Week 5 is done," the sheriff said, pinning up the new wanted posters. "But Week 6 is coming, and in Fantasy Gulch, redemption and ruin are separated by the space between heartbeats."

The sun set behind the mountains, painting the sky the color of dried blood. In the saloon, the survivors gathered to drink and forget. But some things can't be forgotten. Some defeats echo through eternity. And somewhere in the darkness, Mitch was still screaming.

*The wind picked up again, carrying dust and memories and the promise of violence to come. In Fantasy Gulch, there's always another Sunday. Always another showdown. Always another chance to become legend or corpse.*

**Week 5, 2025. Never forget what happened here.**
