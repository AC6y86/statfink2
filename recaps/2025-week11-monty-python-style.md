### Being the Eleventh Part of the Chronicles of the Year Two Thousand and Twenty-Five, As Recorded by Brother Maynard's Fantasy Scrolls

## Scene I: In Which We Set the Stage Most Dramatically (And Then Abandon It Entirely)

*[A medieval castle. Thunder. Lightning. Very, very frightening. NARRATOR enters, trips over a football, recovers with dignity]*

**NARRATOR:** Right! What's all this then? Week 11 of the fantasy football season, and already the peasants are revolting! Though to be fair, they've always been rather revolting, haven't they? Especially Eli's team, sitting there with a measly 79 points like some sort of mathematical tragedy performed by particularly depressed accountants.

But I digress! *[turns to camera]* Oh, hello there. Didn't see you come in. Welcome to this week's fantasy football recap, brought to you by the Knights Who Say "Ni!" and the number 155, which, coincidentally, is exactly how many points Chris scored this week before we remembered that defensive bonuses aren't actually made of cheese.

These warriors of the waiver wire, these gladiators of the gridiron, these... oh, who are we kidding? They're just blokes sitting on their sofas refreshing their phones every thirty seconds like demented hamsters pressing the dopamine button.

## Scene II: The Tale of Sir Chris and the Holy Grail of 155 Points

*[Trumpets sound. A coconut horse gallops across the screen. It's actually just Bruce making clip-clop sounds with two halves of a coconut while reading injury reports]*

Lo and behold! From the mists of mediocrity emerged Sir Chris, wielding the mighty Josh Allen like Excalibur itself! Forty-eight points! FORTY-EIGHT! That's more points than there are varieties of cheese in France! (Editor's note: This is factually incorrect, but we're committed to the bit now.)

"But how," you cry, your voice echoing across the vast emptiness of your mother's basement, "how did Chris achieve such magnificent heights?" Well, I'll tell you, though frankly you could have just looked at the bloody scoreboard yourself, couldn't you? Josh Allen threw touchdowns like a medieval trebuchet launching diseased cattle over castle walls - effective, devastating, and slightly concerning from a public health perspective.

But wait! There's more! Like a late-night infomercial for pointy sticks, Chris also deployed Dak Prescott for 29 points, because apparently having one elite quarterback isn't enough when you're compensating for something. And let's not forget TreVeyon Henderson with 27 points, running through defenses like the Spanish Inquisition - nobody expected him! Though unlike the Spanish Inquisition, he actually showed up when scheduled.

Yet even in triumph, tragedy lurked. Josh Jacobs, Tony Pollard, and RJ Harvey combined for a spectacular zero points, proving once again that fantasy football is just astrology for men who think they understand statistics. Chris's team scored 155 net points after defensive adjustments, crushing poor Eli's dreams like... like... oh, I don't know, like something very crushable. A biscuit, perhaps. A very sad, underperforming biscuit.

## Scene III: The Dreaded Matchup of Doom (Mitch vs Sean: A Tragedy in Three Acts)

*[Enter two PEASANTS, looking confused]*

**PEASANT 1:** I heard Mitch scored 93 points!

**PEASANT 2:** And Sean scored 76!

**PEASANT 1:** That's not very many, is it?

**PEASANT 2:** Well, it's more than the number of shrubberies in this entire production.

**NARRATOR:** Ah yes, the battle of the "We're Not Dead Yet" brigade! Mitch, armed with Brock Purdy's modest 21 points (about as exciting as watching paint dry on a particularly humid Tuesday), somehow emerged victorious over Sean's even more pathetic 76-point effort. It was like watching two elderly knights duel with pool noodles - technically combat, but nobody's really getting hurt, are they?

The true tragedy? Mitch started Chuba Hubbard, Brian Robinson Jr., AND Cooper Kupp - all of whom scored precisely nil, zero, zilch, nada, nothing! It's like he actively tried to lose but couldn't quite manage it because Sean was trying even harder! Meanwhile, Michael Penix Jr. sits on Mitch's injured reserve with a torn ACL, which is somehow still more functional than his active roster decisions.

Cam Skattebo? Also on IR. Terry McLaurin? Out. It's like Mitch's roster is performing Monty Python's Dead Parrot sketch, except instead of a Norwegian Blue, it's his entire receiving corps! "This team is no more! It has ceased to be! It's expired and gone to meet its maker! This is a late team! It's a stiff! Bereft of life, it rests in peace!"

But somehow, SOMEHOW, Sean managed to be worse. It's rather impressive, really, like failing a test when all the answers are written on the board. Sean's team mustered 76 net points, which in fantasy football terms is about as impressive as claiming you've discovered the airspeed velocity of an unladen swallow but forgetting to specify African or European.

## Scene IV: The Middling Middle (Where Dreams Go to Die But Not Quite Completely)

*[A HISTORIAN appears, immediately gets eaten by a rabbit. Another HISTORIAN takes his place]*

**HISTORIAN:** In the middle tier, we find several teams trapped in fantasy purgatory, not quite good enough to win, not quite bad enough to get sympathy points.

Dan's Team faced Cal's Team in what historians will surely ignore completely! Cal emerged victorious with 109.5 points, thanks largely to Bijan Robinson's 25 points. Bijan ran through Dan's defense like King Arthur through the "Bring Out Your Dead" scene - purposefully and with great dignity, despite the absurdity surrounding him.

But Cal's victory was pyrrhic at best! Ashton Jeanty, Emeka Egbuka, and Dylan Sampson - his supposed "depth" - combined for zero points. ZERO! That's the same number of times this recap has made actual sense! It's like having a sword that's very sharp on one end but the other end is made of pudding. Chocolate pudding, granted, but pudding nonetheless.

Dan, meanwhile, scored a respectable 98 points, which in any other matchup might have been enough. But this is fantasy football, where "might have been" is the official motto, right after "Why did I start him?" and "The projections said he'd score 15!"

**Pete the Victorious** crushed Aaron 98-60 in what can only be described as a massacre. Well, it could be described other ways - "a drubbing," "a thorough beating," "Tuesday" - but massacre sounds more dramatic, doesn't it? Sean Tucker exploded for 33 points like the Holy Hand Grenade of Antioch - "One... two... FIVE!" "Three, sir!" "THREE!" Aaron's team, crippled by Jayden Daniels being out, Kyler Murray on IR, and Marvin Harrison Jr. also out, looked like the Black Knight after all his limbs were removed - still talking a big game but fundamentally unable to do anything useful.

Apparently yes, though it defies all logic and several laws of physics.

Bruce, meanwhile, started players who performed about as well as the French government in 1940 - present but not particularly effective. The ghost of Calvin Ridley haunts his IR spot, a constant reminder that some wounds never heal, especially when they're listed as "fibula - Injured Reserve" in your lineup.
