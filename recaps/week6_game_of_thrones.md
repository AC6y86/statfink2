# The Chronicles of Week the Sixth: A Song of Points and Fire
*As recorded by the Maesters of the Fantasy Citadel*

## The Gathering Storm

The ravens arrived at dawn, black wings cutting through the autumn mist as they brought word from the Great Sept of Statistics. Week the Sixth had concluded, and with it came tales of glory and despair that would be sung in the halls of fantasy lords for generations to come. The realm trembled as ancient prophecies began to unfold—prophecies that spoke of Houses rising from the ashes while others descended into the darkness of defeat.

From the high towers of the Fantasy Citadel, the Grand Maester unfurled his scrolls, revealing truths that would shake the very foundations of the Seven Kingdoms of Fantasy Football. "Winter is coming," he whispered, though some houses had already felt its icy grip upon their throats. The average army strength across the realm stood at 95 points—a number that would separate the mighty from the meek, the conquerors from the conquered.

As the sun rose blood-orange over the battlefield, three Great Houses emerged from the carnage with armies exceeding 130 points—a display of dominance not seen since the days of Aegon the Conqueror. Meanwhile, in the shadows, lesser houses counted their dead, their scores barely reaching 50, wondering if the Old Gods had abandoned them entirely. The stage was set for epic confrontations that would determine not just the fate of Week Six, but the trajectory of entire seasons.

## The Triumvirate of Terror

### House Cal: The Dragon Awakens (137.0 Points)

In the western reaches of the realm, Lord Cal stood atop the ramparts of his castle, watching as his dragons circled overhead. With 137 points—the second-highest score of the week—House Cal had unleashed devastation upon their enemies that would be remembered in song and story. The young dragon Caleb Williams breathed fire worth 22 points upon the battlefield, while the twin terrors Drake London and Jaxon Smith-Njigba each scorched the earth for 20 points, their flames intertwining in a deadly dance of destruction.

But it was Bijan Robinson, the Beast of Atlanta, who truly embodied the fury of House Cal's assault. With 23 points, he trampled through enemy lines like a direwolf among sheep, leaving broken shields and shattered dreams in his wake. Even Trevor Lawrence, often dismissed as a lesser dragon, contributed 19 points to the conflagration. Together, they formed an unstoppable force that reduced their opponents to ash and memory.

The scholars would later note that House Cal's performance marked their fourth victory in six battles, elevating them to 688.5 total points for the season—a testament to their growing power. In the great game of thrones that is fantasy football, House Cal had proven they were not merely players, but conquerors destined for greatness.

### House Joe: The King in the North Rises (136.5 Points)

From the frozen wastes beyond the Wall came word of House Joe's terrifying display of power. With 136.5 points—mere whispers away from the week's summit—Lord Joe had marshaled forces that would make even the Night King tremble. Rico Dowdle, the Wildling warrior from Carolina, led the charge with 23 savage points, his battle cry echoing across the frozen tundra as he carved through defensive lines like Valyrian steel through butter.

George Pickens, the Shadow Assassin of Dallas, struck from the darkness for 20 points, each reception a dagger to the heart of House Pete's hopes. Jonathan Taylor, the Iron Bull of Indianapolis, stampeded for 17 points, while Ladd McConkey, the Lightning Lord of the Chargers, added another 17 points of electrical fury to the assault. Together, they formed a brotherhood of destruction that left the battlefield littered with the broken dreams of their enemies.

With this victory, House Joe ascended to joint leadership of the realm with five wins against a single loss, their season total of 735 points standing as a monument to their dominance. In the great hall that night, the bards sang of Lord Joe's tactical brilliance, how he had taken common sellswords and forged them into an army worthy of legend. The King in the North had risen indeed, and all who stood before him would kneel or be destroyed.

## The Great Battles of Week the Sixth

### The Massacre at Chris's Landing: House Chris (122.5) vs House Matt (67.0)

The morning mist had barely lifted when House Chris descended upon House Matt's forces like a plague of locusts. Lord Chris, wielding the triple-headed dragon of Josh Jacobs (25 points), De'Von Achane (25 points), and Dak Prescott (24 points), orchestrated a slaughter so complete that even the crows turned away in disgust. With 122.5 points to Matt's pitiful 67, this was not a battle—it was an execution.

House Matt's lone bright spot came from Bryce Young, the Boy King of Carolina, who managed 21 points in what historians would later call "a heroic last stand in a hopeless cause." But against the overwhelming might of House Chris's war machine, it was like trying to stop a tidal wave with a teaspoon. The 55.5-point margin of victory stood as one of the week's most decisive conquests, a reminder that in the game of thrones, you win or you die—and House Matt had died spectacularly.

The victory elevated House Chris to 5-1 for the season, tied atop the Iron Throne standings with House Joe. With 692.5 total points, Lord Chris had proven that his early season success was no fluke. The whispers in the taverns spoke of a dynasty in the making, of a house that had found the perfect balance between aggression and strategy. Meanwhile, House Matt, despite their 4-2 record and 532 points, was left to lick their wounds and contemplate how quickly fortune can turn in this brutal game.

As the sun set on this one-sided affair, the maesters recorded in their tomes: "On this day, House Chris did not merely defeat House Matt—they broke them upon the wheel of fantasy football, leaving nothing but dust and regret in their wake."

### The Duel of the Desperate: House Mitch (57.0) vs House Dan (52.5)

In the shadow lands where hope goes to die, two battered armies met in what the smallfolk would later call "The Battle of the Broken." House Mitch, with 57 points, faced House Dan's 52.5 in a confrontation that resembled two wounded direwolves fighting over the last scrap of meat in a frozen wasteland. Neither house covered themselves in glory this day, but someone had to emerge victorious from this pitiful display.

House Mitch's salvation came from an unlikely hero—Cam Skattebo, the Forgotten Knight of New York, who exploded for 30 magnificent points in what was otherwise a desert of despair. It was as if the Seven themselves had taken pity on Lord Mitch and sent a champion to prevent total humiliation. Meanwhile, the rest of his army collapsed around him: Puka Nacua (0 points), Brian Robinson Jr. (0 points), Stefon Diggs (0 points), Kareem Hunt (0 points)—a litany of zeros that read like a death toll from a plague-ravaged village.

House Dan's resistance was led by Jalen Hurts, who managed 22 points despite being surrounded by incompetence on all sides. But when your army scores 52.5 points in total, even having the Mountain himself wouldn't save you. The 4.5-point margin of victory for House Mitch was the smallest of the week, a testament to how evenly matched these two struggling forces were in their mutual mediocrity.

Both houses now stood at 3-3 for the season, House Dan with 524 points and House Mitch with 489.5, languishing in the middle of the realm like forgotten bastards at a royal feast. The victory, if it could be called that, kept House Mitch's playoff hopes alive, though barely. As one tavern wench was heard to say: "It wasn't pretty, but in the game of thrones, an ugly win beats a pretty loss every time."

### The Shocking Betrayal at Winterfell: House Bruce (103.0) vs House Mike (50.0)

In what future historians would record as "The Red Sunday," House Bruce descended upon House Mike with the fury of a thousand suns, delivering a 53-point thrashing that left the realm gasping in disbelief. Lord Bruce, commanding 103 points, watched with satisfaction as his generals Drake Maye (26 points) and Daniel Jones (24 points) led the charge, their combined 50 points alone matching House Mike's entire pathetic output.

House Mike's 50 points tied for the lowest score of the entire season—a shameful display that had their ancestors rolling in their graves. Baker Mayfield managed a respectable 19 points, but when your quarterback is the only warrior who shows up to battle, defeat is not just likely—it's inevitable. The rest of House Mike's forces simply failed to answer the call, disappearing like morning dew under the harsh sun of combat.

This crushing defeat dropped House Mike to 1-5 for the season with only 503.5 total points, relegating them to the dungeons of the standings where only the truly desperate dwell. Meanwhile, House Bruce improved to 3-3 with 599.5 points, keeping their slim playoff hopes flickering like a candle in a hurricane. Lord Bruce was heard to declare in the great hall afterward: "Today we didn't just win—we made a statement. Let all who would challenge us remember this day and tremble."

The 53-point margin of victory stood as the week's largest, a monument to both Bruce's tactical brilliance and Mike's complete strategic collapse. In the annals of fantasy football history, this would be remembered as the day House Mike's season effectively ended—not with a bang, but with a whimper that could barely be heard over the sound of House Bruce's victory celebration.

### The Battle of the Fallen: House Pete (89.0) vs House Joe (136.5)

House Pete arrived at the battlefield with hopes of redemption, but instead found only annihilation at the hands of House Joe's relentless war machine. With 89 points against Joe's mighty 136.5, this was less a battle and more a public execution witnessed by the entire realm. D'Andre Swift galloped valiantly for 20 points, but one knight cannot win a war when the rest of your army has forgotten how to hold a sword.

The 47.5-point defeat served as a brutal reminder of House Pete's fall from grace. Once a proud house with dreams of glory, they now stood at 2-4 with 569 points for the season, watching their playoff hopes crumble like the Wall after the Night King's assault. Lord Pete was seen wandering the battlements that night, muttering about injured warriors and cruel fates, but in the game of thrones, excuses are worth less than copper coins.

### The Razor's Edge: House Eli (67.5) vs House Sean (65.0)

In a battle so close that the maesters had to check their scrolls thrice, House Eli emerged victorious by the slimmest of margins—2.5 points that might as well have been a thousand for how they shifted the balance of power. Lord Eli's secret weapon proved to be Patrick Mahomes, the Wizard of Kansas City, whose 32 points stood as the week's highest individual performance by a quarterback, a display of sorcery that left the smallfolk whispering of dark magic and forbidden spells.

House Sean fought bravely, with Justin Herbert (19 points) and Joe Flacco (18 points) combining for a respectable quarterback tandem, but when the dust settled, they had fallen just short. The 2.5-point margin was so narrow that Lord Sean spent the night poring over every play, every decision, wondering what single choice might have changed his fate. Such is the cruelty of the fantasy realm—sometimes kingdoms are won or lost by the length of a sword.

Both houses now stood at crossroads in their seasons: Eli at 4-2 with 523.5 points, still in the hunt for the Iron Throne, while Sean languished at 2-4 with 512 points, their dreams of glory fading like winter light. The victory kept House Eli's hopes burning bright, while House Sean was left to contemplate whether their season was already slipping through their fingers like grains of sand.

### The Culmination at Casterly Rock: House Cal (137.0) vs House Aaron (79.5)

The final great battle of Week Six saw House Cal deliver the killing blow to House Aaron's already diminished hopes. With 137 points—the week's highest total—against Aaron's modest 79.5, this 57.5-point destruction was both merciless and methodical. House Aaron, now 0-6 with a mere 484 points for the season, had officially become the realm's doormat, the house that others used to sharpen their swords before real battles.

Jayden Daniels (24 points) and Jaxson Dart (22 points) fought valiantly for House Aaron, but valor alone cannot overcome a cursed season. When your house has lost every single battle, when the gods themselves seem to have turned their backs on you, even small victories feel like distant dreams. Lord Aaron was last seen staring at the standings scroll, perhaps wondering if it was too late to take the black and join the Night's Watch.

## Prophecies and Portents: The Road Ahead

As the dust settles on Week Six, the shape of the season begins to crystallize like ice on a winter morning. Houses Joe and Chris sit atop the realm with 5-1 records, but their paths to glory are far from assured. The middle of the realm remains a battlefield of chaos. Houses Cal (4-2), Matt (4-2), and Eli (4-2) maintain winning records, but their Week Six performances raise questions. ## The Final Reckoning

As the ravens carry these tales across the Seven Kingdoms, one truth emerges clearer than Valyrian steel: Week Six was a week of separation, of wheat being sorted from chaff, of pretenders being exposed and contenders rising to claim their birthright. The 87-point gap between the week's highest scorer (Cal, 137) and lowest (Mike, 50) stands as the largest such chasm of the season, a statistical monument to the inequality that defines this brutal game.

The realm now knows its true powers. Houses Joe and Chris have proven themselves worthy of the Iron Throne, while Cal demonstrated they have the weapons to strike at any moment. Meanwhile, in the dungeons of despair, Mike, Aaron, and the other fallen houses must confront a harsh reality: their seasons may already be over, their dreams crushed beneath the boots of stronger, smarter, more fortunate lords.

As we prepare for Week Seven, remember the words of House Fantasy: "Points are Coming." For some, those points will lift them to glory, their names sung by bards for generations. For others, the coming weeks will bring only more pain, more disappointment, more evidence that in the game of thrones, you win or you die—and most of you are already dying.
