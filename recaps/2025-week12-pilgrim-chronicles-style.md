# The Pilgrim Chronicles: Twelfth Week of the Settlement
## A Journal of the 2025 Fantasy Football Season, Week XII

---

*Transcribed from the weathered leather journal of Elder Scribeson, keeper of the settlement's fantasy football records, November in the Year of Our Lord 2025*

---

### Prologue: The Gathering Storm

'Twas the twelfth week since our twelve settlements first cast their lots upon this savage gridiron wilderness, and lo, the heavens themselves seemed to tremble with the weight of what was to transpire. As the November winds howled through our humble colonies, carrying upon them the whispered prayers of settlers desperate for playoff berths, we gathered around the flickering candlelight to witness what would become one of the most consequential Sabbaths in our young league's history.

The harvest had been poor for many this week, with the league average falling to a mere 81.25 provisions per settlement--far below our seasonal norm of 94.09 that hath sustained us through prior weeks. Winter's cruel hand reached out early, leaving many a settler shivering in the cold cellar of underperformance. Yet amidst this scarcity, certain pioneers found bounty beyond measure, while others faced the bitter taste of famine. Thus begins our chronicle of Week XII.

The standings entering this fateful week revealed a colony divided: the prosperous northern settlements of Chris and Joe, both perched at 9-2, gazed down from their ivory towers upon the struggling masses. Meanwhile, the beleaguered Aaron settlement languished at 2-9, their playoff harvest all but lost to the frost. 'Tis time now to record the trials and triumphs of each matchup, that future generations may learn from our fortunes and follies.

---

### The Headliner: Joe's Great Feast (138.0 Provisions)

In all my years chronicling these settlements, rarely have I witnessed such abundance as that which blessed Joe's humble homestead this week. With 138.0 provisions gathered--a full 56.75 above the league's meager average--Joe's settlers feasted like kings whilst their brethren scraped for crumbs.

The twin pillars of this great harvest were Jalen Hurts, whose quartermaster duties yielded 30.0 provisions, and the fleet-footed Wan'Dale Robinson, who contributed 20.0 provisions through his receiving endeavors. But 'twas the collective effort that truly built this bounty: James Cook III and George Pickens each delivered 17.0 provisions, whilst even the backup quartermaster Jacoby Brissett managed 14.0 when called upon. Jaylen Warren (11.0) and Christian Kirk (11.0) added their labors to the storehouse, and the defensive fortifications contributed 8.0 provisions of their own.

What made this harvest all the more remarkable was its distribution--no single crop failed, no single field lay fallow. Joe's settlement demonstrated the wisdom of diversified planting, reaping rewards from every corner of the roster. This victory elevates Joe to 10-2, now tied atop the standings with Chris's settlement, though the provision count gives Joe a commanding 1479.5 total to Chris's 1488.5. The race for the top seed remains as tight as a Puritan's collar.

---

### The Matchups: Tales of Triumph and Tragedy

#### Joe's Settlement (138.0) vs. Aaron's Outpost (64.5)
*"The Massacre at Plymouth Rock"*

If Joe's settlement enjoyed a bountiful harvest, then Aaron's outpost suffered the worst locust plague since biblical times. The 73.5 provision differential represents not merely a defeat, but a complete and total devastation--a winter storm that left no structure standing.

Aaron's troubles began with the harsh truth that Kyler Murray remained confined to the Injured Reserve, his foot ailment keeping him from the fields. Without their primary quartermaster, Aaron's settlers turned to anyone who could hold a musket, but the results were catastrophic. The highest contributor was Jakobi Meyers with a paltry 11.0 provisions, followed by Tetairoa McMillan (8.0), Zach Charbonnet (8.0), and Jauan Jennings (8.0). The once-promising Marvin Harrison Jr. yielded nothing, as did Jayden Daniels, Brian Thomas Jr., Tank Bigsby, and Mark Andrews--all contributing zero to the settlement's survival.

This crushing defeat drops Aaron to 2-10, their playoff dreams now as dead as the leaves on the November maples. Meanwhile, Joe's settlers celebrate with vigor, knowing their position atop the standings grows ever more secure. 'Twas a massacre, pure and simple, and Aaron's outpost must now look to next year's planting season.

---

#### Cal's Homestead (124.5) vs. Bruce's Farm (63.0)
*"The Second Great Abundance"*

If Joe's harvest was the greatest of the week, Cal's was surely the second. With 124.5 provisions gathered, Cal's homestead proved that diversification and wise roster management can still yield tremendous rewards even in lean times.

The star of Cal's fields was the young receiver Jaxon Smith-Njigba, whose 28.0 provisions represented the third-highest individual performance across all settlements this week. But Cal's true strength lay in his quartermaster depth: Trevor Lawrence contributed 24.0, whilst Caleb Williams added 21.0 more. The much-discussed Ashton Jeanty delivered 14.0 provisions from the rushing position, and Dylan Sampson added 11.0. Even the defensive fortifications contributed 8.0 provisions, and the trusty kicker Jason Myers provided a steady 7.5.

Poor Bruce, meanwhile, watched his farm yield only 63.0 provisions despite honest effort. Daniel Jones served admirably at quartermaster with 16.0 provisions, and Drake Maye added 14.0. But the rushing corps--led by Chase Brown's mere 9.0--failed to produce the expected yield. Michael Wilson (9.0) and DK Metcalf (8.0) offered modest contributions, but when Brock Bowers manages only 3.0 provisions and Nick Chubb produces nothing, the harvest simply cannot match the demand.

This victory moves Cal to 7-5, firmly in playoff contention, while Bruce slips to 6-6, now walking the precarious middle ground between survival and elimination.

---

#### Mike's Settlement (98.5) vs. Sean's Colony (67.0)
*"The Gibbs Phenomenon"*

In the wilderness of fantasy football, sometimes a single settler can carry an entire village upon his back. Such was the case for Mike's settlement, where Jahmyr Gibbs produced a season-defining 39.0 provisions--the highest individual output of any settler across all twelve colonies this week.

Gibbs's performance was nothing short of miraculous, a one-man harvest festival that single-handedly ensured Mike's survival. But he did not labor entirely alone: Quinshon Judkins contributed 16.0 provisions, Harrison Butker added 10.0 from his kicking duties, and the defense managed 10.0 of their own. Kyle Monangai (8.0), Tez Johnson (8.0), and Baker Mayfield (5.0) rounded out the contributors.

The tragedy for Mike, however, lies in what could have been. Tee Higgins sat with an injury yielding zero provisions, as did Alvin Kamara, Jordan Love, Tyjae Spears, Romeo Doubs, and Mason Taylor. The winter storms of injury devastated nearly half the roster, yet Gibbs's Herculean effort compensated for all.

Sean's colony, meanwhile, simply lacked the firepower to compete. Cam Ward's 22.0 provisions led the way, with Travis Etienne Jr. adding 14.0 and Joe Flacco contributing 11.0 from the bench. But when Justin Jefferson, Rhamondre Stevenson, and Devin Singletary all yield zero provisions, the math simply does not favor victory.

Mike rises to 5-7 with this win, keeping faint playoff hopes alive, whilst Sean falls to 3-9, their season effectively concluded.

---

#### Chris's Village (79.5) vs. Mitch's Encampment (53.0)
*"The Prescott Prophecy Fulfilled"*

Chris's village entered this week atop the standings at 9-2, and while their 79.5 provisions fell below the seasonal average, it proved more than sufficient against Mitch's struggling encampment. The 26.5 provision margin of victory was never truly in doubt.

The foundation of Chris's harvest was Dak Prescott, whose 30.0 provisions from the quartermaster position provided the essential backbone. Sam Darnold, in a supporting role, added 16.0 more, whilst Rashee Rice contributed 11.0 receiving provisions. Josh Allen delivered 9.0, and the defense added 8.0 to the coffers. Even modest contributions from TreVeyon Henderson (3.0), Rome Odunze (3.0), and Jake Ferguson (3.0) helped build the winning total.

'Twas the injured settlers that told the true tale, however: Josh Jacobs, De'Von Achane, RJ Harvey, Tony Pollard, Jameson Williams, and Jordan Addison all failed to contribute, their bye weeks and ailments leaving significant provisions on the table.

For Mitch's encampment, this week brought only sorrow. Kareem Hunt led with 17.0 provisions, Brock Purdy added 11.0, and Tyler Loop kicked for 7.0. But the catastrophic losses elsewhere proved insurmountable: Lamar Jackson produced zero, as did Chuba Hubbard, Brian Robinson Jr., Cooper Kupp, Stefon Diggs, Xavier Legette, Puka Nacua, and George Kittle. Michael Penix Jr. remains on Injured Reserve with his reconstructed knee, and Cam Skattebo also languishes on the injured list. This devastation drops Mitch to 5-7, still mathematically alive but facing an uphill climb.

Chris's victory extends their record to 10-2, maintaining their position as co-leaders of the settlement confederation alongside Joe.

---

#### Eli's Cottage (71.0) vs. Matt's Manor (101.5)
*"The Reversal of Fortune"*

For Matt's Manor, this week brought redemption after a string of disappointing harvests. With 101.5 provisions--a full 20.25 above the weekly average--Matt demonstrated that his settlement still possesses the capability for greatness when the fields cooperate.

Derrick Henry anchored the effort with 19.0 rushing provisions, whilst DJ Moore matched that total from the receiving corps. Christian McCaffrey, perhaps finally shaking off his early-season ailments, contributed 17.0, and A.J. Brown added another 17.0 from his receiving duties. The kicker Andy Borregales provided 9.0, Michael Pittman Jr. added 8.0, and DeVonta Smith contributed 6.0. Bryce Young, in a limited role, managed 5.0 from the quartermaster position.

Eli's cottage, meanwhile, found itself outmatched despite respectable individual performances. Amon-Ra St. Brown led with 17.0 provisions, and Patrick Mahomes contributed 14.0 from his quartermaster duties. Darnell Mooney added 11.0, CeeDee Lamb managed 6.0, and Trey McBride provided 6.0 more. But the injury plague struck hard here as well: Joe Mixon remains Out with his foot/ankle ailment, contributing nothing, and David Montgomery, Joshua Palmer, and T.J. Hockenson all failed to produce.

Matt's victory moves his Manor to 8-4, solidifying a playoff position, whilst Eli falls to 5-7, now facing a desperate battle for the final playoff berths.

---

#### Dan's Homestead (60.5) vs. Pete's Plantation (54.0)
*"The Battle of the Desperate"*

Sometimes the most meaningful contests are not the highest-scoring affairs, but rather the gritty, low-provision struggles between settlements fighting for survival. Such was the case in this matchup, where Dan's homestead edged Pete's plantation by a mere 6.5 provisions in what can only be described as a clash of the wounded.

Dan's victory was built upon the reliable Davante Adams, who contributed 19.0 provisions, and the steady Geno Smith at quartermaster with 14.0. Khalil Shakir added 9.0, and the kickers Brandon McManus (7.0) and Nick Folk (2.5) provided modest support. But the damage lay in the silent fields: Saquon Barkley managed only 3.0 provisions despite his star status, Kenneth Walker III produced just 3.0, Zay Flowers yielded 3.0, and Deebo Samuel, Aaron Rodgers, Kyren Williams, and Blake Corum all contributed absolutely nothing.

Pete's plantation suffered similarly. Matthew Stafford led with 24.0 provisions, a sterling performance under difficult circumstances, whilst Jared Goff added 19.0. But beyond the quartermasters, the cupboard was bare: Nico Collins managed only 3.0, Woody Marks produced 3.0, and the kickers Evan McPherson (5.0) and Michael Badgley (5.0) provided limited support. Brandon Aiyuk remained Out with his knee injury, further depleting the roster's potential.

Dan's narrow victory moves his homestead to 5-7, keeping playoff hopes barely flickering, whilst Pete falls to 6-6, still in contention but running out of time to secure a berth.

---

### Performance Tiers: The Settlement Hierarchy

**The Elite Provisions (100+ points):**
As winter approaches, only three settlements managed to harvest above the century mark this week. Joe's magnificent 138.0 stands alone at the summit, followed by Cal's impressive 124.5, with Matt's respectable 101.5 rounding out the upper tier. These three settlements demonstrated that even in lean times, proper roster management and a bit of fortune can yield tremendous abundance.

**The Middling Settlers (60-99 points):**
Mike (98.5) narrowly missed the elite tier but deserves recognition for Gibbs's heroics. Chris (79.5), Eli (71.0), and Sean (67.0) all fell within the competitive middle range. Aaron (64.5), Bruce (63.0), and Dan (60.5) scraped the bottom of the middle tier, their provisions barely sufficient to sustain their colonies through the cold months ahead.

**The Struggling Masses (Below 60 points):**
At the bottom of this week's hierarchy sit Pete (54.0) and Mitch (53.0), both settlements falling victim to the dual plagues of injury and bye week absences. Their combined 107.0 provisions would have been insufficient to defeat Joe's settlement alone--a sobering reminder of how cruel this wilderness can be.

---

### The Bust Brigade: Those Who Abandoned Their Posts

The winter storms of Week XII claimed many victims among the roster elite. Most egregious were the complete failures from supposedly reliable settlers:

Lamar Jackson of Mitch's encampment produced exactly zero provisions, as did his teammates Chuba Hubbard, Brian Robinson Jr., Cooper Kupp, Stefon Diggs, and George Kittle. Similarly, Aaron's Jayden Daniels, Marvin Harrison Jr., Brian Thomas Jr., and Tank Bigsby all abandoned their posts. Dan's supposedly elite Saquon Barkley managed a pitiful 3.0, whilst CeeDee Lamb of Eli's cottage mustered only 6.0. These failures cost their settlements dearly in the race for playoff positioning.

### Close Calls and Heartbreaks

While many matchups were decided by comfortable margins, the Dan vs. Pete battle stood out as the week's closest contest at just 6.5 provisions. Had Pete found merely 7 more provisions from his silent fields--perhaps from the injured Brandon Aiyuk or the underperforming roster--the outcome would have flipped entirely. Such are the cruel vagaries of this frontier existence.

### Looking Forward: The Path to Survival

With but two weeks remaining before the playoff harvest begins, the standings paint a clear picture of who hath survived and who hath perished:

**Secured Their Place at the Table:**
- Chris (10-2) and Joe (10-2) have all but guaranteed their seats at the playoff feast
- Matt (8-4) sits comfortably in third

**Fighting for Survival:**
- Cal (7-5) holds a strong position but cannot rest

The final weeks shall separate the wheat from the chaff, the survivors from the fallen. May fortune favor the prepared.
