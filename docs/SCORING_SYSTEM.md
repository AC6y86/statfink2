# SCORING SYSTEM

## Roster Composition

Each team scores any player they draft and accumulates a weekly score based on the following 11 players:

- 1 each **Quarterback**
- 4 each **Running Backs**
- 4 each **Wide Receivers or Tight Ends**
- 1 each **Kicker**
- 1 each **Bonus Players**

**Defense:**
- 1 each **Team Defense** (fewest points allowed)
- 1 each **Team Defense** (fewest yards allowed)

The least points allowed and least yards allowed bonuses are each awarded weekly among the drafted defenses. Ties split the 5 points equally (e.g., a 2-way tie pays 2.5 each).

#### Defensive Touchdowns — Exact Award Logic

The Team Defense **IS** awarded 8 points when the defense or kick-coverage unit scores:

- Interception returned for TD
- Opponent's fumble returned or recovered for TD (a takeaway — the fumbling team was on offense)
- Blocked punt / blocked FG / blocked kick returned or recovered for TD
- Muffed punt or returner's fumble recovered by the kicking (coverage) team for TD — a special-teams takeaway

The Team Defense is **NOT** awarded points when:

- An offensive player recovers his own or a teammate's fumble in (or into) the end zone — that is an **offensive TD**: the recovering player gets 8 points as "Touchdown Scored (by any player)". A player who recovers his *own* fumble and scores is already credited with a rushing TD in NFL stats. Examples (2024): Trey McBride wk 2 (teammate's fumble → McBride 8, DEF 0); Tank Bigsby wk 5 (own fumble → rushing TD, DEF 0).
- A kickoff or punt is returned for a TD — that is credited to the individual returner (20 points, per "Kick or Punt Returner" below); the Team Defense gets 0.

**The determining question:** did the ball change possession on the play (or was the kick blocked)? Takeaway and blocked-kick TDs → Team Defense. Same-possession TDs → the individual player who scored.
